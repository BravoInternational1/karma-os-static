The Paradox of Seeking: When the Path Becomes the Destination

I've been reflecting on something that came up during my meditation retreat last week. Our teacher mentioned that "seeking enlightenment is like a wave seeking the ocean" - it already IS the ocean. This got me thinking about how we often create suffering by constantly seeking something we already possess.

In my own practice, I've noticed that the moments of deepest peace come not when I'm striving for some spiritual achievement, but when I simply rest in what's already here. The breath, the awareness, the present moment - it's all already perfect and complete.

How do you balance the motivation to practice and grow with the acceptance of what already is? I'd love to hear your insights on this beautiful paradox.
